Curtis Jackson biography

Curtis James Jackson, aka 50 cent, was born on 6th July 1975 in Queens, New York City. He was brought up in the South Jamaica neighborhood by his mother, Sabrina, who was a drug dealer. When he was eight, his mother was killed, and his grandmother took over the responsibility of bringing him up. When he was eleven, he got into boxing, and he began selling drugs when he was 12 without his grandparents’ knowledge.

While in tenth grade, his attempt to bring drug money and guns to where he schooled at Andrew Jackson High School was caught by metal detectors, and he was arrested. In 1994, he was arrested again after he was caught selling cocaine to undercover police, and he also had a pistol. He was to serve three to nine years; however, he was fortunate and got to serve six months while in boot camp and got his GED. He adopted the name ‘ 50 cent’ from Kelvin Martin, who was a Brooklyn robber in the 1980s. The name was a metaphor for change since he could relate to the person he got it from.

He was, however, dropped when he attempted to release Ghetto Qu’ran and was also blacklisted by the United States recording industry. Thu, he to Canada, where he recorded mixtapes.
